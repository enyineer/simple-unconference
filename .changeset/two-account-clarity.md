Make the two-account model understandable. The conference sign-in now explains that each conference has its own password (separate from an organizer account), detects when the entered email belongs to the conference organizer or an unclaimed invite and shows targeted guidance instead of a flat "wrong password", and offers a prominent "sign in from the main page" path that returns you to the conference after signing in. The join page clarifies that it creates a conference-scoped sign-in, links to the conference sign-in when the email already exists, and skips straight into the conference if you are already signed in. Claiming an invite or joining via link while signed in to an organizer account with the same verified email now links the conference to that account automatically (with an upfront note), so those users never end up with two disconnected logins. The organizer sign-in's error message likewise points out that conference passwords do not work there. And when someone types their organizer account password at a conference sign-in, the server now recognizes it (verified against the organizer credentials, with full double lockout accounting) and explains exactly what happened instead of a generic failure.
