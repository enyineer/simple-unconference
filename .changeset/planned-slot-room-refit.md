Moderators can now "Re-fit rooms" on a planned slot. It's a stable repair, not a re-ranking: only talks whose room no longer fits move - when interest has outgrown the room, a pinned room points elsewhere, room requirements aren't met, or the room is double-booked by a time-overlapping slot. Each misfit is sent to the best-fitting free room (the smallest room that still covers its interest, so bigger rooms stay free), with a single swap against a less-starred talk when nothing free fits. Talks that already fit never move, and rooms occupied by an overlapping slot (including mixer rooms) are treated as unavailable. Genuine pin configuration errors still abort with a conflict and no changes; talks that can't be improved stay put and are reported. Room double-booking is now prevented across the board: auto-assign, hand-placing an unconference session, and hand-scheduling a planned track all refuse a room already used by a time-overlapping slot. When you pick a specific room for a planned track that's taken by an overlapping slot, the error names what's using it (e.g. "Big is already used at 16:00 by 'Keynote' in an overlapping slot"); editing a track already in its room is never blocked. Attendees whose talk is scheduled into, moved within, or removed from a planned slot get a coalesced "Schedule updated" notification (mandatory sessions notify everyone), and moving an unconference placement to a new room notifies the people currently seated there. The agenda response also carries a moderator-only participant count.
